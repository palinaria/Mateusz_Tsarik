The word “Holi” is derived from the Sanskrit term “Holika”, which is associated with the Hindu legend of the demoness Holika and the pious young man Prahlada.

According to the myth, Holika tried to burn Prahlada in the fire, but she herself died, while the young man remained unharmed - thanks to the protection of the god Vishnu. This story symbolizes the triumph of faith and good over evil, and fires are still lit on the eve of the festival - Holika Dahan - as a ritual remembrance of this event.
